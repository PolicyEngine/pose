# POSE interview guide

## Before the interview

1. **Research the interviewee** - Check LinkedIn, their org's website, any prior interactions
2. **Identify segment** - Which ecosystem segment do they represent?
3. **Prepare hypotheses** - What assumptions are you testing with this person?
4. **Schedule 30-45 minutes** - Allow time for depth without being burdensome

## Interview structure

### Opening (2-3 min)
- Thank them for their time
- Brief context: "We're exploring how to grow PolicyEngine's open-source ecosystem"
- Permission to take notes/record
- "No wrong answers - we want to learn from your experience"

### Discovery questions (20-30 min)

**For Users:**
- Walk me through how you currently use PolicyEngine
- What problem were you trying to solve when you found us?
- What alternatives did you consider?
- What's missing or frustrating about PolicyEngine today?
- Who else in your organization or network might benefit?

**For Contributors:**
- What motivated you to contribute (or consider contributing)?
- What barriers have you encountered?
- What would make contributing easier/more rewarding?
- How do you think about the project's governance?

**For Supporters/Funders:**
- What draws you to supporting open-source policy tools?
- How do you evaluate impact of projects you fund?
- What would make PolicyEngine a stronger funding candidate?

**For Competitors:**
- How do you see the landscape of policy simulation tools?
- Where do you see PolicyEngine fitting vs your approach?
- What gaps do you see in the market?

**For Distributors/Partners:**
- How did you decide to integrate PolicyEngine?
- What was the integration experience like?
- What would make the partnership stronger?

### Closing (5 min)
- "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"
- "Who else should I talk to?"
- Thank them and explain next steps

## After the interview

1. **Write up notes within 24 hours** while fresh
2. **Extract 3-5 key insights** - What surprised you? What validated/invalidated assumptions?
3. **Update ecosystem canvas** if this changes your understanding
4. **Add follow-ups to CRM** - Referrals, action items
5. **Update interview log** with completion date

## Tips from I-Corps methodology

- **Don't pitch** - You're there to learn, not sell
- **Ask "why" 5 times** - Dig beneath surface answers
- **Seek disconfirming evidence** - Don't just look for validation
- **Talk less, listen more** - 80/20 rule
- **Capture exact quotes** - "In their words..."
- **Embrace the awkward pause** - Silence often prompts deeper answers
